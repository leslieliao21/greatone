2019 The North Face 100®Ultra Trail Challenge
Race Rules and Regulations

i. General Regulations

The rules and regulations hereby established are to ensure the fairness, impartiality, openness and maintenance of sportsmanship in the 2019 The North Face 100® Ultra Trail Challenge.

(1) Definition
Trail running is a sport-activity which combines running, and, where there are steep gradients, hiking, that is run "on any unpaved surface" including but not limited to mountains, rivers, and hiking trails.

(2) Competition form
The competitions in this event are all timed, all athletes will be ranked according to their finishing time. The athletes who finish their respective event category, 20km, 50km and 100km, will obtain International Trail Running Association (ITRA) points.

(3) Competition categories and allowed participants
10km Beginners (1500 athletes)
20km Advanced (800 athletes)
50km Elites (300 athletes)
100km International Elites (100 athletes)

(4) Awards
The top three athletes in each category will receive The North Face product vouchers regardless of age and or gender. The monetary value of the vouchers will vary depending on the category.

ii. The Organisation

(1) Organiser
The organiser of the event is responsible for the preparation, organisation and implementation of the whole event. The organisation is formed by the following departments: the office, competition team, publicity team, venue & equipment team, security assurance team, logistics & support team and medical aid team.

(2) Referee Committee
Responsible for accepting all appeals during the event and making any final ruling on the appeals. The Referee Committee shall consist of more than one person, including at least one competition official.

(3) Competition Committee
The committee is formed by the following members: competition officials, race director, chief referees and chief route coordinators.

  1. The Competition Officials
    The competition officials are appointed by the organiser. The main duties are to comprehensively coordinate the work among the organiser, the referee committee, the competition committee and the referees to ensure the normal operation of the event and comprehensively supervise the organization and management of the event.

  2. The Race Director
    Responsible for the technical issues in the venue, the main duties are to ensure that the technical planning and management of the competition are carried out properly during the event. Designated by the organiser, its main responsibilities are as follow:
    1) As the highest technical authority during the event, the Race Director has the right to finalise the route of competition and supervise the safety of the competition in accordance with the IAAF Competition Rules and its spirit. Also, the race director could propose to modify, postpone and or terminate the competition according to the safety requirements.
    2) Assist and supervise the work of the chief referee to ensure that the competition complies with the regulations of Ultra Trail Challenge events.
    3) Review the competition routes, items and mandatory equipment to ensure there are no safety hazards. Submit a safety report to be reviewed by the competition committee.
    4) No intervention is allowed on the competition except for potential safety hazards.
    5) Sign comments on the final report submitted by the chief referee.

  3. The Chief Referee
    With the highest authority in the field of responsibility (including the quarantine area, the check-in area, the competition area, the rest area and any other areas related to the safety and fairness of the game, including the area for spectators and media). The specific responsibilities are as follows:
    1) Restrict the behaviour of media units and other staff appointed by the sponsor.
    2) All aspects of normal competition are within the jurisdiction of the chief referee.
    3) The chief referee shall preside over all meetings attended by referees and other organisers, and technical meetings attended by the sponsor and athletes.
    4) The chief referee does not directly participate in the specific refereeing of the competition, which, along with the achievement judgments, are generally to be done by other referees, but he or she may participate in various referee-related work if he or she considers it to be necessary.
    5) Before the start of the competition, the chief referee is obliged to brief the route referee and other referees on the application of all rules and regulations.
    6) After the game is over, the referee should submit to the organizer a detailed event report and an evaluation report of the performance for each referee.

  4. The Chief route coordinator
    The chief route coordinator and the route coordinator are designated by the event organiser to form a route coordination team, mainly responsible for the design of the race route. The main responsibilities are as follows:
    1) Plan the design and maintenance of all routes before the game.
    2) Set up and place race markers, checkpoints and other related equipment as required.
    3) Maintain and make sure the race routes are clean.
    4) Check the technical standards and safety issues of each race route, and make suggestions to technical representatives or chief referees for all technical issues in the competition area.
    5) Verify the specific information of the race route and ensure the accuracy of the data.
    6) Test each route before the game and set the cut-off time for each category.
    7) After the competition is over, the chief route coordinator shall submit to the organizer a report of the event and an evaluation report on the performance of each route coordinator.

iii. Registration Regulations

(1) Qualification

  1. Athletes can register as an individual or team.

  2. Athletes must be between the age of 18 and 60 years old on the competition date.

  3. Athletes in any of the individual categories will not be allowed to participate in other categories.

  4. Athletes must have good physical fitness. If necessary, the organiser may require the athletes to provide a medical certificate and electrocardiograms.

  5. Athletes suspended by any international or domestic sport organisations are not eligible to participate in this event.

  6. All registered athletes must be confirmed by the organiser before participation.

iv. Timing and Results

(1) General regulations
The ranking of each division will be determined by the time that each athlete completes the race. As per international standards, the official digital timer of each event will start counting after the starting gun fires, and the timer will stop when the athlete passes the finish line. The competition committee may determine the interval and number of athletes in each batch depending on the total number of athletes.

(2) Completion and Withdrawal

  1. Completion means that the athlete finished the whole event as required within the cut-off time. Athletes who finish the competition will be included in the official ranking.

  2. If an athlete needs to withdraw from the race, he or she must immediately notify a member of the staff or referee at the nearest checkpoint and wait in the same place for further instructions from the race organiser. Any athlete who leaves the race without a clear notice to a member of the staff or referee will be disqualified and penalised.

(3) Results

  1. The end time is counted after the athlete's chest crosses the finishing line, and the timing is accurate to 0.01 seconds. If a digital timer is used, the timing shall be subjected to the time displayed on the time sheet, and the ranking shall be based on the result of the gunshot.

  2. Athletes will be ranked according to the time when they complete the whole competition. If more than one athlete achieves the same result, their rankings are tied and the next ranking is vacated. Their names will be listed in the same ranking on the time sheet, but the names are listed in the order of the departure time.

  3. If the athlete misses a checkpoint, his or her result will be invalid. If the checkpoint time check is missed due to technical problems (checkpoint chip reader is damaged or the digital timer is not working, etc) and it is also approved by the referee and the chief referee that the athlete has passed the checkpoint, the performance will be still considered valid.

v. The Route Design

(1) The route design

  1. The route design should fully reflect the impartiality of the competition and the characteristics of trail running. The route design should fully test the athlete's running abilities.

  2. There should not be sections endangering the safety of the athletes in the whole route.

  3. No more than 30% hardened pavement such as asphalt or cement should be present throughout the whole route.

  4. Downhill sections shall not have more than 30% decline per kilometre on average.

  5. Checkpoints should be set on the route, and the athletes should pass them as required.

  6. The route should be designed to avoid nurseries, sowing areas, fields with crops, railways, driveways and other areas where access is restricted.

  7. Clear markings shall be placed throughout the whole route. The markings should be clear and easy for athletes to follow. It is advised to have staff guides at fork sections and or in complex terrains where athletes may get confused.

  8. All sections must be selected and designed in accordance with environmental protection principles to avoid damage to the environment.

(2) Distance of the route

  1. When determining the distance of the competition, in addition to the factors of the division, the complexity of the competition area, the season, the start time of the race and other factors that may affect the game should also be considered.

  2. The total cumulative upgrading height of each route is about 7% of the total length of the route, and 20% error is allowed. In special cases, the standard can be adjusted, and athletes need to be specially informed before registration.

vi. Aid Stations

(1) Sufficient water and food should be available at the starting point, the end point and the athlete's resting area.

(2) Location of the Aid Stations should be set according to the length and characteristics of the route. Generally, the distance between each Aid Station shall not exceed 15 kilometres.

(3) The water and food at the Aid Station should be placed in a position easy for athletes to access, and all the outer packages should be removed in advance in order to avoid congestion and or waste the athlete’s time.

(4) The energy supplies prepared and carried by the athletes may be handed over to the staff in advance, and be placed at the Aid Station for the athletes to use.

vii. Equipment

(1) Equipment provided by the organizer
According to the race requirements, the following equipment will be provided for the athletes during the competition:

  1. Race bib

  2. Digital timing chip

(2) Athletes' own equipment

  1. Clothing and shoes suitable for trail running. For the safety of the participants, it is forbidden for all runners to participate in the competition without wearing running shoes (such as barefoot, wearing slippers, etc.). Disposable cups are not available throughout the event and all contestants are required to bring their own water supplies.

  2. Mandatory equipment:
    Participants in the 100km and 50km divisions are required to go through mandatory equipment inspections in the check-up area. The mandatory equipment includes:
    (1) 2 sets of headlights and 2 sets of extra batteries
    (2) A hydration backpack
    (3) Compass
    (4) Safety pins
    (5) Windproof jacket
    (6) Thermal emergency blanket
    (7) Mobile phone, power cord and power bank (athletes in the 50K category must install on their mobile phones the application for the tracking system. No athlete is allowed to start until the command centre confirms the signal of their positioning.)
    (8) Race bib (requires emergency contact number)
    (9) Two timing chips (one has been attached to the back of the bib and the other is hung in an obvious position outside the backpack)
    (10) A Water bottle or hydration pack (capacity 500c.c or above)
    (11) Simple first aid kit (e.g. ointment, bandage, and other medical equipment)
    (12) Light raincoat
    (13) Whistle

  3. Precautions
    1. After crossing the finish line or after withdrawal from the race, the digital timing chip should be returned.
    2. The digital timing chip should be properly kept. If it is lost during the competition, the result will be invalid.
    3.After crossing the finish line and having rested for a while, please take all your belongings with you..

viii. Medical Support and Rescue

(1) Medical aid stations and medical staff will be properly stationed at the starting and finishing line, athlete resting areas and along the route in certain locations.

(2) During the race, if athletes show any abnormal symptoms, the medical staff of the event have the right to conduct physical examinations for the athletes. The athletes who are questioned or examined must immediately suspend the competition and cooperate with the medical staff for examination.

(3) According to the examination results from the medical staff, the referee reserves the right to terminate the athlete's competition or force the athlete to rest for certain amount of time.

(4) Athletes may apply for rescue at any time during the competition.

ix. Official BIB

(1) During the event, all athletes on the race route must wear the official race bib provided for the competition according to the following requirements:

  1. The race bib should be pinned in an obvious position above the waist and below the neck.

  2. Contestants are not allowed to remove the race bib anywhere on the race route (except when examined by the medical staff).

(2) Any team or athlete that changes, obstructs, smears or modifies the race bib provided by the organiser, will result in the athlete being disqualified.

(3) The logos of the athlete sponsors must be placed neatly and firmly on the clothing. The organizer reserves the right to remove any trademark that are considered indecent, inappropriate or substandard. The total size of the trademark shall not exceed 10*10 cm, otherwise the penalty will be imposed according to Article X.

x. Penalties

Any team and or athlete who violates the rules of the competition could and will be subjected to a verbal warning, yellow card, disqualification, suspension or ban. The participating units can be subject to corresponding penalties depending on the seriousness of the circumstances and the levels of responsibility. The following are penalties for violations of rules.

(1) Verbal warning
The purpose of verbal warning is to remind athletes of the possible violations they may commit and to show the referee's "concerns."
Referees may issue a verbal warning in the following circumstances:
1) When an athlete unintentionally commits a violation
2) When the referee considers an athlete likely to commit a violation
3) When athletes are acting against their best interests.
After being issued a verbal warning, the athlete shall proceed according to the instructions of the referee.

(2) Yellow card
A yellow card is a penalty imposed against violations committed by an athlete or a team. Two yellow cards in a single race will result in disqualification from the event.

  1. Intentional violation
    1) Interfering race progress of other athletes
    2) Failing to obey the referee's instructions immediately
    3) Inappropriate language or behaviour against any referee
    4) Failure to proceed according to the designated race course
    5) Gaining advantage from acceptance of external help
    6) Refusing inspection and or a check-up from the race medical staff

  2. Non-compliant use of the official BIB
    1) Team members without BIB will have race results nulled
    2) Failure to wear BIB according to requirements (including cutting, modification and non-matching number of BIB) will be subject to a 2-minute penalty

  3. Acts that affect the environment 1) Actions that ruin the environment 2) Disposal of garbage during the race

(3)Penalty, disqualification and having the race result voided.

  1. Overview
    Disqualification and voidance of race results are penalties imposed against severe violations committed by an athlete and the team.

  2. Penalties
    Referees may disqualify athletes or void race results in the following methods:
    1) Waving the red flag or showing a red card;
    2) Calling the athlete's number;
    3) Announcing it after the race

  3. Reasons for disqualification or voidance of race results
    1) Conducts in a manner that violates the sport ethics
    2) Refusal to obey referee's instructions
    3) Filing of fraudulent claims
    4) Intentional disruption to the race
    5) Intentional removal or alteration of race markings
    6) Intentional vandalism of race equipment
    7) Violation of race rules to own benefit, or affecting other athletes as a result
    8) Wearing race outfits that violate rules

  4. Penalties related to race results
    1) No result will be counted if the timing chip is lost during the race
    2) Team members who miss or fail to successfully clock their time at the checkpoint (run over the electronic mat) will be subject to a 2-minute penalty once verified by the route referee. Race result will be voided if it is confirmed that the runner never passed through the checkpoint

  5. Award ceremony
    Winners who are not present at the award ceremony (except for medical reasons or with the approval of the chief referee) where there is no change in venue or time will have the winning titles removed. If there is a change to the award ceremony, the organizer will reserve the prize.

(4) Suspension or banning
Participants who impersonate another runner, commit fraud or pass the finished line repeatedly for race ranking, prize or finisher medal will be placed on the violators list and suspended from TNF100 races for 2 years on the first offense, or banned from TNF100 races for a life time if recurring or in the case of severe offense.

xi. Appeals

(1) Appeals for disqualifications
Any appeal for the disqualification of an athlete or a team should be submitted in writing to the Referee Committee before the competition ends, and the decision on the appeal will be made before the official results of the competition are announced.

(2) Appeals filed by an athlete team
Any appeal filed by an athlete team against any other team members or the referees shall be submitted to the Referee Committee orally or in writing within 30 minutes after the completion of the competition.

(3) Appeals about the results
Appeals for timing errors should be submitted within 15 minutes after the referee's announcement of the unofficial race results. Appeals about official competition results may be submitted to the Referee Committee in writing by the club, association or athlete himself within 30 days after the competition.

(4) Letter of appeal
The letter of appeal can be obtained from the referee and filled out truthfully as required.

(5) Appeal fees
A fee of NT$2,000 should be paid when submitting the letter of appeal. The fee will be refunded if the appeal is successful.

(6) Process of appeal

  1. The letter of appeal should be truthfully filled out as required, signed and approved by the person making the claim, the team leader or the coach, and then submitted to the Referee Committee in writing within the time limit specified in the above provisions.

  2. After accepting the appeal, the Referee Committee shall immediately convene relevant personnel for a hearing.

  3. If either party is absent from the hearing, the Referee Committee reserves the right to make a ruling in the absence of that party.

  4. With the approval of the Referee Committee, the representative of the claimant may attend the hearing.

  5. The hearing will not be held in public.

  6. The letter of appeal is read by the Director of the Referee Committee.

  7. Sufficient time should be given to the parties to state their views.

  8. The witnesses of both parties have three minutes to make their presentations.

  9. The Arbitration Committee makes a ruling by the majority principle based on the testimonies of the parties.

  10. The ruling is immediately announced and takes effect in real time, and ruling of the Arbitration Committee is final.

xii. Anti-doping

(1) The Organizer adopts the International Anti-Doping Regulations and resolutely opposes the use of stimulants.

(2) Athletes who register, prepare for or participate in the competition in any capacity must abide by and obey this regulation.

xiii. Interpretation

The 2019 TNF100 Organizer is responsible for the final interpretation and revision of this rule.

Supplementary Rules (published date: Mar.20,2019)



The following supplementary rules have been established to ensure the fairness and safety of the race.

Participating athletes are advised to comprehend and comply with the following rules:

1. Neutral assistance

1-1 Participants are required to prepare adequate amount water and supplies for the race. Also, check and resupply when passing through the checkpoints.

1-2 Should an emergency occur outside the aid station, participants may seek supply from race officials (referees, rescue team, volunteers etc.) or public merchants (neutral third parties). For the fairness of the race, race officials will record the runner's ID number and apply a 10-minute penalty for every supply made (e.g. refilling of water or food).

1-3 Participants are not permitted to seek private supply (including but not limited to water, food, assisted stretch and massage) outside the designated aid stations. However, friends and family members may offer private supply at designated checkpoints (CP).

Violators of this rule will be subjected to a verbal warning, penalty or disqualification depending on the severity.

2. Safety first principle

2-1 Medical staff have the authority to temporarily suspend participants from race for a health check-up, which participants may not refuse for any reason.

2-2 If the physician considers it necessary to suspend or disqualify a runner from the race for rest or treatment due to health reasons, the runner must comply with the instructions of the medical staff and may not refuse in any way.

2-3 Part of the race course may be altered due to undesirable weather condition. Please follow the race officials' instructions and pay attention to race announcements (including but not limited to SMS, broadcast, aid station notices, trail sign, etc.)

2-4 Courses and contingent plans of the current race have been filed with the local government. All trail signs, labels, shelters and ropes are properties of the organiser and are critical to the runners' safety. Any attempt to remove, alter, vandalize or obstruct the above will be considered a malicious act against other participants and the organiser, and will result in disqualification or even legal consequences.

3. Rules about equipment

3-1 Referees have the authority to perform mandatory equipment inspection at any location along the race course. The purpose of the inspection is to ensure the runners' safety and the fairness of the race, and participants may not refuse in any way.

3-2 All divisions other than the 10KM are allowed to use trekking poles, subject to the following rules:

3-2-1 Attention must be paid to runners in front and behind, athletes will responsible for any injuries caused to others because of the trekking poles. The organiser encourages participants to use protective covers on the trekking poles.

3-2-2 Participants of the 50KM and 100KM divisions are entitled to use trekking poles throughout the course, but must pay extra attention to the safety of nearby runners.

3-2-3 20KM runners may use trekking poles after CP1.

3-3 Head lamp in the mandatory equipment list must be carried at all times. Flashlights or mobile phones with LEDs are not allowed as replacement for the head lamp. All athletes must make sure to have backup batteries available.

3-4 Emergency blanket in the mandatory equipment list is an important gear for keeping warmth outdoors, and cannot be replaced with ordinary blankets, windbreaker, raincoat or any other item. The emergency blanket must be carried at all times.

Violation of this rule will be subject to a verbal warning, penalty or disqualification depending on the severity.

4. Leave No Trace principles

4-1 Do not damage plants, trees, cultural heritage or hurt animals along the course.

4-2 Do not smoke or light fires of any kind on the trails.

4-3 Respect the workers, the local residents, tourists and hikers. Be considerate and share the trails. Do not yell, push or shove others. Violations of the above rules will be subject to a verbal warning, penalty or disqualification depending on the severity of the actions.

4-4 There are mobile toilets deployed throughout the race course and at the check points, so avoid leaving waste in the nature. If there is a need to discharge in the wild, please dig a hole and bury properly.

4-5 Do not leave any form of rubbish along the race course, including but not limited to food wrapping, bottles, fruit peels, seeds or food remains. Violators will be issued a warning on the first offense and disqualified on the second offense.

4-6 Participants are required to have their own water bottles or hydration packs ready at the aid station, and shall not take water flasks away from the aid station.

Further details that are not covered in the race rules and supplementary rules may be announced over the official portal.
