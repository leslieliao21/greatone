HIGHLIGHT

This orienteering activity is similar to a "Treasure Hunt" for families.

This activity will be conducted in groups. First, a knowledgeable instructor will teach the basics and some safety instructions. Then the instructor will lead a guided practice so everyone is familiar with orienteering. Once all questions have been answered all the groups will have the opportunity to discuss and study the map in order to decide which direction to go.

Just like a treasure hunt, participants find ways to explore each of the different locations along the map route. This will let all the participants to experience and have a better understanding of this international sport.

What is Orienteering?

Orienteering originated in Norway and Sweden, where there are vast forests, lakes and grasslands. Residents of those areas could easily get lost without precise orienteering skills. Thus, the need to cultivate a good sense of direction and map-reading skills from an early age. Later, it evolved into a globally popular sport. With a map and compass, participants search for the target positions in the mountains and complete the assigned task as quickly as possible.

Taiwan attracts many fans for orienteering as well. In addition, the World Games 2009 Kaohsiung, the 2009 Summer Deaflympics (also known as the 21st Summer Deaflympics in Taipei) and the hosting of the 5th Asian Orienteering Championships in Taiwan in 2016 made orienteering gradually popular in Taiwan, and it has become a way for many families to get closer to nature with their kids!

To put it simply, orienteering is a sport testing the physical strength and intelligence of the contestants, in which, the contestants judge the direction with the compass, then punch at all the checkpoints marked on the map, and the fastest finisher wins the game!
