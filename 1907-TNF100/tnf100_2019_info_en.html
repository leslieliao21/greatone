1. Introduction:

The North Face 100km is back and this time with new challenges and new adventures for all its participants. Elite athletes from all over the world will gather with one objective in mind: become the King of the Mountain. Runners will have the opportunity to run through some of the most beautiful and scenic mountains of Taiwan, and for this the ethics of Leave No Trace (LNT) are mandatory.

Implementation of Alternate Routes：

The race organiser will decide to implement new routes in the case of the following conditions:

1. Continuous heavy rain for more than 2 days before the race day or extreme rainfall one day before the race (accumulated rainfall is more than 80mm in the last 24 hours, or hourly rainfall is more than 40mm)

2. Continuous rainfall (2.6mm-8mm per hour) during the event ( Apr.20 to 21. )

* ote: If an alternate route is implemented for the 20KM group the ITRA points will not be obtained.

8. Participants: International athletes, running clubs, ultra-athletes and trail running enthusiasts are all welcome to join the 2019TNF100km.

Registration Method: Only Online registrations is accepted.

Athletes wishing to sign up for the 100KM/50KM/20KM Categories must read the Registration Requirements and submit the supporting documents to the organizer to the following email address: r4taiwan@gmail.com . Please send the documents within one week after registering and include your order number and the athlete’s full name in the e-mail.

Be aware that your registration will be cancelled if the requirements are not met or the supporting documents are not submitted. The applicant is responsible for any processing fees if refunds are needed.

Registration Requirements:

(1) 100Km International Elite Group Competitors must provide the organizer any of the following race certificates:
1) Official Finisher Certificate of any trail running race for 50 KM or more completed within the last two years;
2) ITRA score: Must have accumulated at least 15 points under the new system within the last two years, with at least 12 points accumulated from two or at most three races under the new system.

(2) 50Km Elite Group
The competitor must provide the organizer with any of the following race certificates:
1) Official Finisher Certificate of any trail running race for 21 KM or more completed within the last two years;
2) Official Finisher Certificate of any full marathon (42.195 KM) or long distance road races completed within the last two years.

(3) 20Km Advanced Group
The competitor must provide the organizer with any of the following race certificates:
1) Official Finisher Certificate of any trail running races for 21 KM or longer completed within the last two years;
2) Official Finisher Certificate of any half-marathon (21.0975 KM) or long distance road races completed within the last two years.

(4) Age Restrictions
*Participants in the 100KM: Eligible for participants aged 18 or above, born before 2001.
*Participants in the 50KM: Eligible for participants aged 18 or above, born before 2001.
*Participants in the 20KM: Eligible for participants aged 18 or above, born before 2001.
*Participants in the 10KM Eligible for participants aged 18 or above, born before 2001.
Children (born after 2001) interested in trail running can also register if accompanied by a participant over the age of 18 on the event day.

(5) Event Information

  1. Please register using a valid identification document. National ID or Passport. Please make sure the information provided is correct and assess your own fitness and abilities in advance as the name and division cannot be changed once registration has been completed.

  2. Registrations are not transferrable. Exchanging or giving the running BIB to other runner is not allowed. Any athlete running with a BIB different than his or hers will be held legally liable for any accidents and the runner will be disqualified from the race.

  3. In order to protect the interests of the participants and for safety reasons no running buddies are allowed in the race course. If the details provided at the time of registration do not match the National ID Card or Passport the details on the official document shall prevail.

  4. After completing the Online registration and payment has been made the system will automatically send a confirmation e-mail to the participant. If no e-mail is received, please check the registration details on the “Check Registration” section of the registration website.

  5. To encourage greater public participation, a team registration form can be downloaded for groups of 20 or more athletes.

  6. Once registration is completed, please e-mail the required documents and order number to the following email address r4taiwan@gmail.com for review.

  7. Refund requests:

    (1) To apply for a refund after payment has been made, please provide the bank name, bank code, branch name, account number and account name at the Apply for Refund page.

    (2) Refund applications made before 2019/2/22 18:00 will be refunded in full after deducting a NTD$100 processing fee + wired transfer fee (NTD$30).

    (3) Refund applications made before 2019/3/22 18:00 will be refunded 70% of the paid amount after deducting a NTD$100 processing fee + wired transfer fee (NTD$30).

    (4) No refunds will be accepted after March 22, 2019.

    (5) All refunds will be transferred to the accounts specified by the applicants on April 1, 2019.

    Note: Due to limited spots we encourage to register before the registration quota reaches its limit. No further registrations will be accepted once the quota is full. Registration details and payment should be done before the given deadline to avoid missing out.

16. General Gear and Mandatory Gear:

  1. The long course and high difficulty means participants must wear suitable sports clothing and running shoes. For safety reasons, participants must wear running shoes. Athletes running barefoot or with sandals during the race will disqualified immediately.

  2. Hydration packs/Water flasks.

  3. Change of clothing: T-shirt, exercise pants

  4. Long or compression socks are recommended due to the tall vegetation along the mountain trails.

  5. Rain jacket, gloves and a hat.

  6. Food: Energy bars, gels or other food alternatives. Assistance from running partners or crew members is not allowed during the race. Therefore, participants MUST carry their own supplies between aid stations.

  7. Drinks: Regular sports drinks.

  8. First-aid Kit: Antacids, Aspirin, Lip Balm, Blister medication, Pain killer, towel, eye drops, antibiotic cream, bandage, band aid.

  9. Mandatory Gear List for 100K and 50K

    (1) 2 head lamps and 2 sets of spare batteries

    (2) Trail Running backpack

    (3) Compass

    (4) Safety Pins

    (5) Waterproof jacket (light rain jacket is not permitted)

    (6) Emergency Blanket

    (7) Mobile phone, power cable and power bank. In the 50K division a real-time position tracking system must be installed on the mobile phone. The command centre will only start the race once it can track the position of all participants.

    (8) Race BIB (must have the emergency contact’s phone number)

    (9) Two timing chips (one is glued to the back of the race BIB, and the needs to be placed on an obvious location on the backpack)

    (10) Water bottle or hydration pack (Capacity of at least 500c.c)

    (11) Basic medical supplies (e.g. medicated cream, bandages, band aid …)

    (12) Whistle

    ※ 100K and 50K participants must complete their inspection at the participant check-in area before they can enter the race.

  10. 20K Mandatory Gear List

    (1) Water bottle or hydration pack (Capacity of at least 500 c.c.)

    (2) Race BIB (must show the emergency contact’s phone number)

    (3) Two timing chips (one is glued to the back of the race BIB, and the needs to be placed on an obvious location on the backpack)

    Top 3 athletes of all categories must wear The North Face 100® commemorative event shirt when they go on stage for the award presentation or they will be disqualified.
